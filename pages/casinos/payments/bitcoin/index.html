Most Common Coin Types

As technologies develop, crypto casino platforms need to adapt to the changing landscape of digital currencies.

  • BTC and Bitcoin Cash

    Bitcoin, or BTC for short, started the whole peer-to-peer, decentralised digital currency trend in 2008. While its price has been volatile, some predictions say that it’s going to break into six figures in a couple of years.

    When the Bitcoin user base grew and the speed of transactions became insufficient, the developers created Bitcoin cash (BCH), which allows for larger block sizes and transactions. Most of the Canadian Bitcoin casino websites that accept BTC also accept BCH.

  • Ethereum

    The second-largest digital coin that crypto casino sites offer is Ethereum. While Bitcoin’s initial purpose was to disrupt national currencies and accepted payment methods, Ethereum aimed to promote its decentralised application platform. Although Ethereum is also a cryptocurrency, it’s not a direct competitor of Bitcoin.

  • Litecoin

    Unlike Ethereum, Litecoin development started based on BTC technology. The coin was established in 2011 by MIT engineer Charlie Lee. Litecoin aims to process transactions in 2.5 minutes instead of the 10 minutes that Bitcoin takes.

  • Dogecoin

    Dogecoin was initially created for fun, but today it’s one of the more accessible cryptocurrencies. The price of one coin is as little as 0.002 USD. This price point makes the currency very convenient for smaller transactions. Unlike Bitcoin and Litecoin, there is no limit on the number of Dogecoins in circulation.

  • Monero

    Monero is gaining popularity because of its killer feature – a distinct way of handling transactions. It breaks the transaction amount into pieces and then, by using the ring signature feature, makes the parties involved in the transaction disappear in a sea of other unrelated sources. These two combined render the transactions untraceable and unlinkable.

  • Dash

    When it was launched, Dash was aimed at anonymity and privacy. Since then, it has changed into the first cryptocurrency you can use for daily purchases.

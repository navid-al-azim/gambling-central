Information on addiction and problem gambling

Addiction and problem gambling are serious issues that can cause financial, social, and emotional problems for individuals and their families. In Canada, various resources are available to help individuals struggling with gambling addiction.

If you or someone you know is struggling with problem gambling, there are several Canadian websites that can provide information and support, including:

CAMH - The Centre for Addiction and Mental Health is Canada's largest mental health and addiction teaching hospital, offering clinical care, research, education, and advocacy services to support individuals and families affected by mental health and addiction issues.

Gamblers Anonymous - A self-help organization for individuals who struggle with gambling addiction. Anyone in need can attend meetings or talk to someone via the hotline.

RNG - Responsible Gambling Council is a non-profit organization based in Canada that aims to prevent problem gambling and promote responsible gambling practices. The council provides research, education, and resources for individuals, communities, and the gaming industry to minimize the harms associated with gambling.

GamblingTherapy.org - An online platform that provides free and confidential support and resources for individuals affected by problem gambling worldwide, including in Canada. The website offers online counselling, self-help tools, and a moderated support forum to help individuals and their loved ones manage problem gambling and related issues.

It is important to note that these websites are not meant to diagnose or treat gambling addiction, but rather to provide resources and support for those who need it.
